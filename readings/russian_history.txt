-Early Russian History

"Ivan the Terrible" by Robert Payne (Narrative Non-Fiction)
A detailed biography that explores Ivan IV’s life, reign, and his impact on Russia.



-The Romanov Dynasty

"Peter the Great: His Life and World" by Robert K. Massie (Narrative Non-Fiction)
A comprehensive and engaging biography of Peter the Great.

"The Winter Palace" by Eva Stachniak (Historical Fiction)
Focuses on the rise of Catherine the Great from an insider’s perspective at the Russian court.

"Catherine the Great: Portrait of a Woman" by Robert K. Massie (Narrative Non-Fiction)
An in-depth look at Catherine the Great's life and her influence on Russian history.

"The Romanov Empress" by C.W. Gortner (Historical Fiction)
A narrative about Maria Feodorovna, mother of the last Tsar, Nicholas II.

"Nicholas and Alexandra: The Classic Account of the Fall of the Romanov Dynasty" by Robert K. Massie (Narrative Non-Fiction)
A detailed account of the final years of the Romanov dynasty.

"The Last Days of the Romanovs: Tragedy at Ekaterinburg" by Helen Rappaport (Narrative Non-Fiction)
An in-depth look at the final days and execution of the Romanov family.



-The Russian Revolution and Civil War

"A People's Tragedy: The Russian Revolution: 1891-1924" by Orlando Figes (Narrative Non-Fiction)
A comprehensive history of the Russian Revolution.

"Doctor Zhivago" by Boris Pasternak (Historical Fiction)
A classic novel that explores the impact of the Russian Revolution and Civil War.
The Stalinist Era

"Stalin: The Court of the Red Tsar" by Simon Sebag Montefiore (Narrative Non-Fiction)
A detailed narrative of Stalin’s reign, focusing on his inner circle.

"The Whisperers: Private Life in Stalin's Russia" by Orlando Figes (Narrative Non-Fiction)
Personal accounts revealing the private lives of people under Stalin’s regime.



-World War II and the Soviet Era

"The Gulag Archipelago" by Aleksandr Solzhenitsyn (Narrative Non-Fiction)
An account of the Soviet labor camp system based on Solzhenitsyn's experiences.

"City of Thieves" by David Benioff (Historical Fiction)
Set during the Siege of Leningrad in World War II, a gripping narrative of survival.

"Secondhand Time: The Last of the Soviets" by Svetlana Alexievich (Narrative Non-Fiction)
Oral history capturing experiences of people who lived through the collapse of the Soviet Union.



-The Cold War and Beyond

"Red Famine: Stalin's War on Ukraine" by Anne Applebaum (Narrative Non-Fiction)
Examines the Holodomor, the man-made famine in Soviet Ukraine.

"The Secrets We Kept" by Lara Prescott (Historical Fiction)
Set during the Cold War, intertwines the lives of CIA operatives and the story behind "Doctor Zhivago."



-Broad Historical Overview

"The Romanovs: 1613-1918" by Simon Sebag Montefiore (Narrative Non-Fiction)
A sweeping history of the Romanov dynasty, covering all Romanov rulers.

-Additional Recommendations
"The Nightingale" by Kristin Hannah (Historical Fiction)
Set in France during World War II, tells the story of two sisters who resist the German occupation, giving a broader context of the era.

"The Winter Queen" by Boris Akunin (Historical Fiction)
A mystery novel set in late 19th-century Russia, following a young detective.
